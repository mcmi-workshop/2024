Keynotes

Dr. Abhijit Das — Tense of Learning Representation for Multimodal and Crossmodal Learning

This talk will explore the evolution and technological advancements in multimodal and crossmodal learning, including challenges, types of co-learning (parallel and non-parallel data), and multimodal fusion strategies. It will also cover the temporal aspect in multimodal data processing, with applications in emotion recognition, NLP, HCI, and robotics. Generative AI and its impact on MCMI will be highlighted, along with future directions in security and ethical considerations.

Dr. Abhijit Das is an assistant professor at BITS Pilani Hyderabad. Previously, he has worked as an assistant professor at Thapar University, as a visiting scientist at the Indian Statistical Institute, Kolkata, as a researcher at the University of Southern California’s Viterbi School of Engineering Information Sciences Institute, as a Post-Doc Researcher at Inria Sophia Antipolis– Méditerranée, France and as a Research Administrator at the Faculty of Software Engineering & IT at University Technology of Sydney (UTS), Australia. Before his PhD, he worked as an Associate Software Engineer at IBM India Pvt Ltd. and as a Lecturer at distinguished universities. He has completed his PhD from the School of Information and Communication Technology, Griffith University, Australia. He is an accomplished machine learning and computer vision researcher with more than 15 years of research and teaching experience. He is presently pursuing an investigation on learning representations and human analysis employing facial and corporeal-based visual features. During his research career, he has filed patents, published several scientific articles in conferences (CVPR, WACV), journals (IEEE TIFS, IEEE TCSVT) and a book chapter and received several awards from the IEEE, and other scientific organisations. During his career, he has received research funding from several government and private agencies such as ISRO, the Indian Army, and DST. Dr Das is involved in organising scientific events such as international conferences, workshops, competitions and special sessions at conferences, and has served as a guest editor for special issues, area chair for (WACV 25, Fg 24, 25)t, technical program committee member (AAAI) for several reputed international conferences and reviewer of top-ranking journals. He is also the winner of the IBM research-sponsored ECCV 2018 BEFA competition.
